History and Background of the Library

The Kalutara Public Library is a welfare institution that serves an invaluable purpose in the era by providing information, knowledge, and educational resources that support the aspirations, expectations, and informational requirements of all significant children and adults. Responsible service is practiced by this Kalutara Municipal Council-governed institution through the implementation of its mission and objectives in a manner consistent with its status as an institution. Subsequently, from the old building known as Clement Wijayaratne Hall, which was formerly situated on this property, this public library was relocated to a tiny room in the left corner of the premises, where the Kalutara Bodhi Temple is presently situated. For that reason, Wijayaratne Hall was recommended. Afterward, this library was relocated to the city council building, where the archives are presently located, and then returned to the city council-owned UNICEF facility. Mr. Chamli Gunawardena, who was a municipal councilor at the time, recognized the necessity for a durable bullock of superior quality in light of the progressive enhancements to library resources. Consequently, the old Clement Wijayaratne structure was entirely demolished and reconstructed to accommodate the library under the supervision of engineers and architects Edward Reid and Geoffrey Bawa. By virtue of this, Mr. Lil Gunasekara, who was in office as District Governor at the time, inaugurated the new public library building on October 19, 1969.
